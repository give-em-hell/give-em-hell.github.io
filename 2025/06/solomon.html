Solomon

Solomon! Solomon! Solomon! Solomon! Solomon!

I love you Solomon this one proud well-built man

Yet I cannot feel your beautiful face or glance upon its visage

It is obscured, I look beyond, for something perhaps to see you for you

Ay Solomon rid me of this zhuk these flaming cinders and coals

Which so brand me and burn at my sides

Speak to me but refrain from biting, freezing

Speak to me for I am three a gloomy person.

Ay Solomon you see it all, you may weigh your words with mastery

Look into me a point behind intently so that you may have no distinct limit

Year after year I await the day where you will gaze behind me once more

And six six I will realize you have never forgotten me.

I love you Solomon and I wish I must never cover your face

With this thick canvas or shroud that I must burn all down

I will you to arrive to appear in front of me I know I must never realize never be enlightened

For I love you Solomon for in the eyes of others you are just S yet to me you are indelible.
