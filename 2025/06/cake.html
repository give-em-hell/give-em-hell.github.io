Cake-Cutting

Cut the cake and do it thusly:

You cut, and I choose.

Drag your knife across the center

But act on the fact that I will take the larger slice for myself.

As such you shall be moral. The scales

should be held straight simply because I would take

the heavier end.

Cut the cake and do it thusly:

You cut, and I choose.

Stop your knife here.

You see, for you to be fair, for you to be

justified in your existence,

your subsistence and your useless consumption,

the larger slice needs to be mine.

Cut the cake and do it thusly:

You cut, and I choose.

Stop your knife here – at the river.

Think about it. You need to – want to

be fair. You want to play it safe.

You cut, I choose.

That is the only fair way to cut cake.
